I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Cognitive Neuroscience at Dartmouth College, advised by [Peter Tse](https://sites.dartmouth.edu/peter) and [Patrick Cavanagh](https://cavlab.net/). My research asks: <i> when the world out there is cluttered and ambiguous, how does the brain construct a subjective percept — and why does perceptual experience sometimes diverge from physical reality? </i>

I study this using illusions and bistable displays that put different motion and position signals in competition, combining psychophysics, eye-tracking, and fMRI to characterize how the brain weights competing inputs and what this reveals about the computational architecture of visual integration.

Previously, I earned my B.A. and M.A. at Yonsei University with [Min-Shik Kim](http://cognition.yonsei.ac.kr/), and conducted research at the Korea Brain Research Institute with [Su Keun Jeong](https://sites.google.com/view/skjeong/home).
 
For more details, check out my [CV](https://eunhyechoe.github.io/cv).
